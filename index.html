Research Summary

I am broadly interested in cryptography and its intersection with quantum computing and complexity theory. My current research studies questions such as:

  • How can one party (non-)interactively prove statements to another untrusting party? [Proof Systems]
  • How can we make sure such proofs reveal nothing else? [Zero-Knowledge Proofs]
  • How can we make such proofs efficiently checkable? [Probabilistically Checkable Proofs (PCPs)]
  • How can we reduce the communication between the prover and the verifier? [Succinct Arguments]
  • How can parties compute joint functions without exposing their private inputs? [Secure Multiparty Computation (MPC)]

Previously, my undergraduate journey exposed me to other interesting research topics across different areas including number theory, machine learning, security, and privacy.
